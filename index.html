Autonomous Navigation for UGVs, UAVs, AMRs, etc.

Assist UGVs (or AMRs) in navigating unknown environments by avoiding obstacles through real-time reactions, similar to human driving, instead of relying on pre-built maps. This approach utilizes simple yet effective stereo depth sensors to create depth maps for obstacle avoidance, avoiding the need for expensive, complex sensors. By integrating basic odometry controls (in combination with GPS), the system enables the vehicle to reach its destination while avoiding obstacles, without relying on AI-driven systems. It performs effectively in dynamic environments, reacting to nearby obstacles rather than mapping the entire environment at each step.

Next Generation CT Injector

A comprehensive redesign of the CT injector and its components to meet user demands for high flow rates. Key improvements in the injector pump design enable compatibility with a full range of injection protocols, considering factors such as patient gender and cardiac output and other physiological attributes. Enhancements also focus on minimizing patient line failures by pre-stressing tubes to withstand high-volume and high-flow-rate conditions, particularly for organs like the heart and brain, where the Contrast Medium attenuation peak is critically narrow.

About Me

Hi, I am Vomsheendhur Raju, but you can call me Vom. Born in Pondicherry, India, I have always had a passion for mechanical engineering and technology. I began my academic journey at the College of Engineering, DIT, Dehradun, before transferring to North Dakota State University (NDSU) in Fargo, USA, where I earned my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering in 2016. After graduation, I worked as a Mechanical Engineer in the sheet metal fabrication industry, where I supervised design, machine programming, testing, and quality control processes, ensuring efficiency and precision at every stage. Additionally, I gained valuable experience in Research and Technology (R&T) Systems with a medical device company, contributing to the development of NGI and other advanced engineering projects for interventional cardiologists. This experience expanded my understanding of innovative technologies, control strategies, and their applications in the medical field. I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at North Dakota State University, currently in the final stages of completing my dissertation, where my research focuses on developing optimal navigation algorithms for unmanned ground vehicles (UGVs). My work is dedicated to advancing robotics and autonomous systems by enhancing the ability of machines to navigate complex environments safely and efficiently. As I near the completion of my doctoral studies, I am eager to apply my expertise in robotics, automation, and mechatronics to contribute to innovative, high-impact projects in cutting-edge technologies.
